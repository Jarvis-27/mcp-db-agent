# PlainQuery — Advertising / Go-to-Market Roadmap

A few honest framing points before the roadmap — I'm Claude Code, optimized for software engineering, not a marketing specialist. My knowledge ends January 2026. What I'm giving you below is the consensus playbook for early-stage dev tools, not paid-marketer expertise. Treat it as a starting map, not a prescription.

Also: **at your stage, "advertising" is the wrong mental model.** Solo founders trying to get from 0 → 100 users almost never win with paid ads — they win with content, community, and direct outreach. Save the ad spend for when you have product-market fit signal and a paid plan that monetizes.

---

# The product (so I'm answering the right question)

Reading from your repo: PlainQuery is an MCP server that lets people ask their database questions in plain English via Claude Desktop / Cursor / ChatGPT / VS Code Copilot. Free + Pro tier via Stripe. Hosted, multi-tenant, OAuth-capable.

Audience to target first: **developers and analysts at small-to-mid teams who already use an AI coding/chat tool and would otherwise be writing ad-hoc SQL.** Don't try to sell to "enterprise" yet — you don't have the surface (SSO, SOC2, etc.) for it, and chasing enterprise burns 6-month sales cycles you don't have.

# Phase 1 — Foundation (week 1-2)

Things you need running *before* any campaign so you can measure what worked.

| What | Tool | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Product analytics | **PostHog** (free up to 1M events) or **Plausible** ($9/mo) | Know where users drop off in onboarding |
| Email capture | **Loops.so** or **Beehiiv** | Most signups won't convert immediately — keep a waitlist/newsletter |
| Public roadmap / changelog | **Canny** (free tier) or just a `/changelog` page | Builds trust + lets users feel heard |
| Live chat | **Crisp** (free) or just an email link | One sentence of feedback from one user beats 100 page views |
| Demo videos | **Loom** (free) or **tella.tv** | Static screenshots don't sell this product — motion does |
| Docs site | **Mintlify** or **Fumadocs** | Better than burying docs in Notion |

Concrete first artifacts to make:
- A **60-90 second Loom** that shows: connect a Postgres DB → ask "show me my top 10 customers" in Claude Desktop → answer appears. That single asset is your single most-shared marketing piece for the next 6 months.
- A **landing page hero** with this Loom embedded above the fold (not below).
- Three **example questions with screenshots** ("Show me churn this month", "Which products spike on weekends", etc.) on the homepage — concrete > abstract.

# Phase 2 — Launch wave (week 3-5)

Pick **one** launch week. Bundle all of this into ~5 days.

1. **Show HN** post on Hacker News — Title format: `Show HN: PlainQuery – Ask your Postgres in plain English from Claude Desktop`. Post Tuesday-Thursday 8-10am Eastern. Be in the comments live for 4+ hours. Reply to every single comment.
2. **Product Hunt launch** — schedule a Tuesday or Wednesday. Recruit ~10 friends to upvote in the first hour (real people, no fake accounts). Have screenshots, the Loom, and a tagline ready in their format.
3. **Anthropic's MCP server registry** — submit a PR to `modelcontextprotocol/servers` repo on GitHub. Being listed there is high-quality traffic. Also list at **mcpservers.org**, **smithery.ai**, and **glama.ai/mcp/servers** (these are the main MCP-focused aggregators as of late 2025).
4. **AI tool directories** (low effort, long tail of traffic):
   - `theresanaiforthat.com`
   - `futuretools.io`
   - `aitools.fyi`
5. **One launch post on Reddit** — pick *one* subreddit, not five. `r/dataengineering` or `r/SaaS` are reasonable. Read their self-promo rules first; some require a value-first post not a launch announcement.
6. **Twitter/X thread** — 6-8 tweets, mostly screenshots and short clips of the Loom. Tag `@AnthropicAI` and `@AnthropicSDK`. Cross-post the same thread on LinkedIn (rewritten in LinkedIn voice — more "I built this because…" less "shipping!").

Expected outcome: 200-2000 visitors, 10-100 signups, 1-10 active users. Adjust expectations down if you have no audience yet. This is fine.

# Phase 3 — Content compounding (week 4 onward, ongoing)

Paid ads decay the second you stop. Content compounds. Write **2 long-form posts per month**, minimum, for the next 6 months:

**Topic templates that work for this product:**
- "I gave Claude read-only access to our Postgres. Here's what happened in 30 days."
- "10 questions you'd never write SQL for but always wanted to ask your database"
- Comparison post: "Claude + MCP vs Hex vs Metabase for ad-hoc data questions" (be honest — comparison posts that aren't shilling perform best)
- Technical deep-dive: "How we sandbox SQL generated by an LLM before running it" (this also doubles as a hiring/credibility signal)
- "MCP for non-technical teammates: a 5-minute setup"

Publish on:
- **Your own blog** (under `plainquery.in/blog`) — owns the domain authority
- Cross-post a teaser to **dev.to**, **Medium**, **Hashnode** (link back to original)
- One **Twitter thread** per post (5-tweet condensed version)
- Submit each one to **HN** (rate-limited — don't submit your own stuff weekly), **Lobste.rs**, the right subreddit

Tools:
- **Claude Code or Cursor** to draft + edit posts (you're already inside it)
- **Buffer** or **Typefully** for cross-platform scheduling
- **Ahrefs Webmaster Tools** (free) for SEO sanity checks

# Phase 4 — Direct outreach (always, but especially week 1-12)

This is the highest-leverage activity at < 100 users. Most founders skip it because it feels awkward. The ones who don't skip it grow.

Tactics:
- Find **5-10 people per week** on Twitter/LinkedIn/HN who recently posted about "writing SQL is tedious" or "I wish I could ask my database in plain English" or who tweeted about Claude Desktop / Cursor. Reply (don't DM) with a one-line: "Built something for exactly this. Free tier, no signup wall. plainquery.in if curious." Don't pitch — offer.
- Find **5 Postgres-focused newsletters** (`Postgres Weekly`, `Bytebase Blog`, `Crunchy Data blog`) and pitch a guest post or sponsor mention.
- DM **5 MCP-tutorial YouTubers** (the niche is small enough that they'll respond) offering to be featured. Free Pro plan to anyone who covers it.
- Post weekly in 2-3 **Slack/Discord communities** where your audience hangs out: MCP Discord (Anthropic's), `r/dataengineering` Discord, `Hacker News` Slack archives (look for an active one).

# What NOT to do at this stage

- ❌ Google Ads / Twitter Ads / Meta Ads — your CAC will exceed your LTV until you have pricing/conversion data
- ❌ Paying for sponsored Product Hunt slots
- ❌ Hiring an SEO/marketing agency
- ❌ Building features because "X persona wants Y" — wait for repeat requests
- ❌ Cold email blasts (you'll burn `noreply@plainquery.in`'s sender reputation; Resend will flag you)
- ❌ Translating to 5 languages "for international reach" — not yet
